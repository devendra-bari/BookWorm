A Tree Grows in Brooklyn : Betty Smith




Rating : 4 / 5


Summary :-

Betty Smith's classic 1943 novel, "A Tree Grows in Brooklyn" is a story that focuses on the growth of Francie Nolan from childhood to early adulthood. It also revolves around her parents and brother as they struggle to survive in the tenements of Brooklyn in the early 20th century.

A Tree Grows in Brooklyn comprises of such an amazing coming of age story. The protagonist Francie Nolan is a young, sensitive, and idealistic. Her bittersweet childhood in the slums of Williamsburg is filled with compassion and cruelty, laughter and heartache, crowded with life and people and incident. Such an amazingly written story, the life of the Nolans is overwhelming, sublime, heartbreaking, uplifting and unforgettable.

This book expertly depicts and captures a unique period in American history whilst also highlighting universal elements of the human experience.


Review :-

"A Tree Grows in Brooklyn" is a called a classic for a reason: It's beautifully written, insightful, and Francie's story is timeless.

My most recent "classic" to be checked off my list! I can truly understand why this is such a beloved classic that has enchanted millions of readers for the last eight decades! The raw honesty in the portrayal of the Nolan family and their struggles and their connectedness is what will make people keep coming back and back again to this story!

Betty Smith did an exceptional job with the book. However, there are too many adult components of this turn-of-the-century novel to make it appropriate for youngsters. Plus, reading this book after some life experience adds to the appreciation of the story and characters.

I just really enjoyed reading it and noting many great insights. It's really an wonderful and fantastic book & deserves a rating of 4/5.
